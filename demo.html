Hong Kong Football in 2024

Summary

Hong Kong successfully reached the final tournament of AFC Asian Cup after a 55-year absence. In preparation for the tournament, Hong Kong pulled a shocking 2–1 victory over China beating them for the first time in 29 years during the friendly match held in Abu Dhabi. Hong Kong then kicked off their 2023 Asian Cup campaign against UAE in which they lost 3–1 in the opening match where Chan Siu Kwan scored the 1,000th goal in the AFC Asian Cup history. They eventually finished the campaign at the bottom of their group after losses against Iran (1–0) and Palestine (3–0). After the Asian Cup, Hong Kong then continue their 2026 World Cup Qualifiers campaign, consecutively suffering losses versus Iran and Uzbekistan, leading to its elimition in this tournament. In September, Hong Kong travelled to Oceania facing Solomon Islands and Fiji, and also facing Liechtenstein in October, marking a milestone as Hong Kong travelled to Europe for the first time in history.
